A Conversation with a Maasai Elder

Published on June 22, 2024 by Yusuf

The air was cool and the sun was setting over the Maasai Mara, casting long shadows from the acacia trees. We were welcomed into the village not as tourists, but as guests. It was here we had the privilege of sitting down with Mzee (Elder) Ole Saitoti, a man whose face holds the wisdom of generations.

On Tradition and the Youth

We asked him about the challenges of preserving Maasai culture in a rapidly changing world. He spoke not with sadness, but with a calm resilience.

"The world changes, and we must change with it," he said, gesturing towards the children playing nearby with a worn-out soccer ball. "But changing does not mean forgetting. A tree can grow new branches, but it must keep its roots deep in the soil. Our roots are our traditions, our respect for the land, and our community."

He explained that while many young Maasai now go to school and work in cities, they still return for ceremonies, still speak the Maa language, and still hold deep respect for the elders and the old ways. The cell phone in a young warrior's hand doesn't erase the knowledge passed down through generations; it simply adds a new layer.

Tourism as a Double-Edged Sword

We were curious about his view on tourism. His perspective was balanced and insightful. He sees both the opportunity and the danger.

"For a long time," he explained, "the big hotels took the money, but we dealt with the consequencesâ€”the land, the animals. Now, with community-based tourism, we have a voice. We can share our culture on our own terms. We can use the money to send our children to better schools, to build a new well."

He paused, looking out at the vast plains. "The right kind of visitor is one who comes with an open heart, not just an open wallet. Someone who wants to listen, not just to see. That is the kind of tourism that helps us keep our roots strong."

Leaving his village, we felt we had gained something far more valuable than a photograph. We had gained a small piece of understanding, a new perspective on the delicate balance between the past and the future.
